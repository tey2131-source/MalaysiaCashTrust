2. Core Finding

Lending Trust vs Capital Market Trust

$

Short-Term Lending Model

How ZICO IPT 3 & UBB OT 2 operate

  • →Trust capital deployed as loans to borrowers
  • →Profits = interest earned on loans
  • →Fixed return range (e.g. 8–9%) — not market-variable
  • →Trust charges borrowers ~12–15%, pays donors 8–10%
  • →2–5% spread = trust company's fees & margin
  • →Governed by SSM / Trustee Act 1949 only
  • →No SC licence required (if lending only)
↗

Capital Market Investment Model

SC-regulated unit trusts & fund management

  • →Trustee invests in securities, bonds, equities
  • →Returns = market-driven, variable, disclosed daily via NAV
  • →Capital Markets Services Licence (CMSL) mandatory
  • →Full prospectus, fund fact sheet published
  • →SC can investigate, freeze assets, enforce
  • →Independent custodian holds assets separately
  • →No guaranteed fixed return — honest risk disclosure
KEY
FINDING
ZICO IPT 3 and UBB OT 2 are money-lending trusts. The fixed profit ranges, absence of NAV disclosure, no fund manager, and no prospectus are all hallmarks of lending — not capital market investment. Under the SC Practice Note issued May 22, 2026, lending trusts fall outside SC jurisdiction (exempt from CMSL) — which means lower investor protection but current regulatory legitimacy.

The Madoff Parallel

Bernie Madoff's USD 65 billion fraud ran for 30+ years precisely because he was his own custodian. He produced fake statements and trade confirmations — and nobody independently verified the assets existed.

With lending-based cash trusts in Malaysia today, the trust company self-reports loan performance with no mandatory external verification.

RISK
The moment an independent custodian had been required of Madoff, the fraud would have collapsed immediately.
5. Legal Safeguard

Section 12, Trustee Act 1949 (Act 209)

Built-In Lending Safeguard

2/3 LTV Rule — Loans on Property Security

A

Independent Valuation

Loan must be made based on a report by a qualified surveyor or valuer instructed independently of any owner of the property.

The valuer cannot have any connection to the borrower.

B

2/3 LTV Cap

Loan amount must not exceed two-thirds (66.7%) of the property value stated in the report.

Max Loan
66.7%
Buffer
33.3%

33% equity cushion before capital is at risk

C

Valuer's Advice Followed

The loan must be made under the advice of the same surveyor or valuer expressed in the report.

Trustee cannot override the valuer's recommendation.

What This Reveals

✓

Loans are almost certainly property-secured (bridging loans, property development, secured lending against land/buildings)

✓

The 33% equity buffer means property must fall more than a third in value before donor capital is at risk

✓

More conservative than unsecured lending — a real asset backs each loan

✗

Donors have no mechanism to verify that S.12 conditions are actually being followed on every loan

6. Risks

Risk Analysis

⚠ Loan Book Opacity

No obligation to disclose loan book composition. Donors cannot verify credit quality, concentration risk, NPL ratio, or actual leverage. A unit trust fund publishes daily NAV — these products do not.

⚠ Ponzi Risk

If the loan book deteriorates, returns can only come from: (1) eroding principal, or (2) new donor funds — the classic Ponzi mechanism. Consistent high-yield payouts without transparent loan performance data is a structural red flag.

HISTORY
Malaysia's 1986–1988 bank cooperative collapse involved 32 cooperatives, 4 banks, RM 9.4B — all attracted funds with high-return promises outside normal banking regulation.

⚠ 20% Early Withdrawal Penalty

Applies to the full setup amount for the entire 3-year tenure — not just remaining months.

⚠ No PIDM Coverage

8. Conclusions

Findings & Due Diligence

Summary Findings

✗

Lending trusts, not capital market. Returns from loan interest. No SC licence, no NAV, no fund manager.

✗

No independent custodian. Trust company wears multiple hats — control, deployment, and self-reporting.

!

Act 209 S.12 is good in principle — property-secured, 2/3 LTV — but donors cannot verify compliance.

✗

20% penalty on full capital for entire tenure traps donors even if warning signs appear.

✓

ZICO IPT 3 has meaningful estate liquidity edge — 14-day death payout vs UBB's end-of-term.

!

Regulatory tightening ahead — New Trust Companies Bill pending; SC watch list active.

Due Diligence Questions to Ask

Q

Who are the borrowers? What are their credit profiles?

Q

What is the current non-performing loan (NPL) ratio?

Q

Is the trust independently audited? Can I see the latest audited accounts?

Q

What is the maximum single-borrower exposure?

Q

What happens to the loan book if the trust company is wound up before year 3?

Q

Has any SC or SSM investigation or enforcement action been taken against the company or directors?

Q

S.12 Compliance: Is every loan property-secured with an independent valuation? Can you confirm all loans comply with the 2/3 LTV cap under Act 209?

Q

Custodian: Is there an independent custodian or third-party auditor verifying the loan book? If not, what independent oversight exists?
